7 Investigating the automorphism group

Let G be a finitely generated group. Then the term γ_cG of the lower central series is fully invariant subgroup of G. Thus every automorphism α∈ Aut(G) induces an automorphism φ_c∈ Aut(G/γ_cG). We obtain a homomorphism ν_c: Aut(G)-> Aut(G/γ_cG), α↦α_c. This homomorphism map the inner automorphism Inn(G) onto Inn(G/γ_cG) and thus we obtain a homomorphism

\nu_c\colon{\rm Out}(G)\to {\rm Out}(G/\gamma_cG).

Similar, for every d≤ c, we obtain a homomorphism μ_c,d: Out(G/γ_cG)-> Out(G/γ_dG). Since ν_d = ν_c∘ μ_c,d, this yields that

{\rm im}(\nu_d)\leq \ldots\leq {\rm im}(\mu_{c,d})\leq {\rm im}(\mu_{c-1,d}) \leq \ldots \leq {\rm im}(\mu_{d,d}) = {\rm Out}(G/\gamma_dG).

This sequence can be used to guess the shape of im(ν_d) and therefore to guess the shape of Out(G)/kerν_d. The \AutPGrp-Package can be used to compute the images im(μ_c,d) if the abelian quotient of G is elementary abelian. For further details we refer to [EH].

7.1 Usage

7.1-1 AutomorphismGroupSequence
‣ AutomorphismGroupSequence( PcpGroup )( function )

if the abelianization of PcpGroup is elementary abelian, this method computes a list of the images of the outer automorphism group of G/γ_cG in Out(G/γ_dG) for any d≤ c with Out(G/γ_dG) being still solvable. More precisely, the entry {\bf a[i][j]} denotes the image of Out(G/γ_j+1G) in Out(G/γ_i+1G). In the following example we consider the nilpotent quotients of the Grigorchuk group and compute its outer automorphism group sequence.

gap> G := ExamplesOfLPresentations( 1 );;
gap> A := AutomorphismGroupSequence( G, 5 );;
[1,2]: ab [ [ 2, 1 ] ]
[1,3]: ab [ [ 2, 1 ] ]
[1,4]: ab [ [ 2, 1 ] ]
[1,5]: ab [ [ 2, 1 ] ]<P/>

[2,3]: ab [ [ 2, 1 ] ]
[2,4]: ab [ [ 2, 1 ] ]
[2,5]: ab [ [ 2, 1 ] ]<P/>

[3,4]: id [ 16, 11 ]
[3,5]: ab [ [ 2, 2 ] ]<P/>

[4,5]: ab [ [ 2, 2 ] ]
